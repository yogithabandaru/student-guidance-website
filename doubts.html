Q1. I am from a non-CS background. Can I survive in IT?

Yes. Many successful IT professionals started with zero coding knowledge. First year is meant for building basics, not judging intelligence. Consistency matters more than background.

Q2. Is first year very important?

Yes. First year builds your foundation in programming, logic, and discipline. Bad habits formed now affect later years.

Q3. Do marks matter more or skills?

Both matter. Marks help in shortlisting; skills help in cracking interviews. A balance is ideal.

Q4. Which programming language should I start with?

Start with C or Python. Do not jump between languages. Strong basics in one language are enough.

Q5. How many hours should I practice coding daily?

1 hour daily is sufficient if done consistently. Quality practice is more important than long hours.

Q7. When should I start thinking about placements?

From first year itself. Not preparation, but awareness and skill building.

Q8. Do companies expect perfection?

No. Companies expect problem-solving ability and learning attitude.

Q9. Are projects compulsory for placements?

Yes. Projects show practical knowledge and initiative. Even small projects matter.

Q10. Will AI replace engineers?

No. AI replaces tasks, not thinking engineers. Engineers who use AI wisely will grow faster.

Q11. Should I depend completely on AI tools?

No. AI is a support tool. Fundamentals must come from your own learning.

Q12. I feel slow compared to others. What should I do?
